## What I Learned Today

### 1. Data Transmission

Types of personal data:
- Volunteered data — explicitly shared by user
  (social media profiles, photos, posts)
- Observed data — captured without active sharing
  (location data, browsing behavior)
- Inferred data — calculated from other data
  (credit scores, behavior patterns)

Hacker relevance: All three are used during
reconnaissance on a target.

A bit = smallest piece of data. Can only be 0 or 1.
This is called binary.

3 methods of signal transmission:
- Electrical signals — data as electricity 
  through copper wire (ethernet cables)
- Optical signals — data as light pulses
  through fiber optic cables (very fast)
- Wireless signals — data through air using
  radio waves, microwave or infrared (WiFi)

---

### 2. Bandwidth and Throughput

Bandwidth = maximum capacity of a connection
(how much data CAN flow)

Throughput = actual amount of data flowing
right now (usually less than bandwidth)

Latency = the delay — time it takes for data
to travel from source to destination

Common bandwidth measurements:
- Kbps = thousands of bits per second
- Mbps = millions of bits per second
- Gbps = billions of bits per second

Simple analogy:
- Bandwidth = size of a water pipe
- Throughput = water actually flowing through it
- Latency = time for water to reach you
